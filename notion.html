What is Notion

Notion is not just for Note, it can be used to manage everything related to your life. Users can easily create various types of content including text, images, videos, and attachments in Notion and rearrange or organize them as needed.

Additionally, Notion provides collaboration capabilities that allow multiple people to edit and discuss within the same document simultaneously. Moreover, Notion offers a comprehensive open platform that caters to various custom needs.

I will show you Notion in three minutes, and you will immediately know what Notion can be used for.

First, let's talk about the structure of Notion. Just like in Google Drive, you can have folders within folders, infinitely extending downwards. Similarly, in Notion, you can have pages within pages.

On the left sidebar, you can see the top-level pages, and you can create an infinite number of top-level pages here. For example, pages for different departments.

After entering a page, you can see a breadcrumb at the top that shows you the current path location. Inside, you can continue to create pages as extensions.

This page can not only create other pages but also write text.

When you select a text, a small formatting window will appear, which allows you to adjust the text style.

The concept of Notion's page is composed of blocks, and the blue in the screen represents the selected block, which can be freely dragged and moved or called for more functions.

By typing the / symbol, a function call menu will appear, and clicking on it will insert the selected function to the block.

There are many functions displayed on the picture, which can be roughly divided into AI functions, text editing functions, media insertion, databases, embed third-party functions, etc.

It may seem a bit complicated at first, but you will gradually become familiar with it. I won't go into further details for now.

The small functional window that appears when you enclose text in a box not only allows you to adjust the text style, but also allows you to comment on the specific text segment.

You will see a discussion thread appear on the right side of the screen.

The "Add comment" feature at the top of the page is for discussion related to the entire page.

Lastly, every page in Notion can easily be shared with permissions. You can invite team members or external users, set viewing or commenting or editing rights, and even allow non-logged-in visitors to view the page. Collaboration becomes very easy with this feature.

The above is a 3-minute introduction to the basic functions, which is enough to let you know what Notion can do. With almost infinite flexibility, you can apply it to various situations.

What is Notion template

Every Notion user can create project management, daily task, financial, calendar, knowledge base, notes, and other different scenarios according to their own needs and make them public as Templates for other users to duplicate.

You can purchase or obtain templates for free on the public platform, like Gumroad, or create templates yourself and share them with others.

When you enable "Share to web", it means that you agree to share the template with uninvited viewers.

The "Allow duplicate as template" below indicates whether or not the viewer can duplicate this template.

Assuming you are using Notion through a web browser, the web link copied directly from the address bar can only be accessed by invited Notion users.

On the other hand, the "Copy web link" option here allows non-logged-in visitors to browse the content.

When this page has been enabled with "Allow duplicate as template," the "Duplicate" option will appear at the top of the visitor's screen. Clicking on it will allow them to duplicate it to their own Notion account.

Comparison between Notion and other Tools

On the market, each type of management tool has its great advantages and has become a mature solution in its field.

Notion combines all the advantages of the above tools. It can use various tools, start discussions, store files, and most importantly, it eliminates the need to use various tools scattered everywhere, as everything can be done within Notion.

Let's first take a look at what are the common management tools.

  • Trello → Card-based task management, which allows you to clearly see which stage your tasks are at and also allows you to expand discussion threads for individual items.
  • Jira → Mesh concept project management, which allows you to see which team members are responsible for each project and which projects each team member is responsible for. It also defines stages and status for each project and produces analysis reports.
  • Slack → Business chat software for internal team communication, which keeps a complete record of conversations. You can create multiple groups to discuss different topics.
  • Dropbox → Document writing and cloud file storage.
  • Google Drive → Cloud folder concept for storing files and various documents.
